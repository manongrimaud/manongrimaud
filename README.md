# Manon Grimaud

**Philosopher · Literary Editor · Researcher in Philosophy of Language & AI**

📍 Paris 

## About

I am a philosopher (ENS de Lyon) and literary editor at Gallimard (2025–2026).
My research focuses on the philosophical underpinnings of large language models —
working at the intersection of philosophy of language, philosophy of technology,
and AI ethics.

This is not a technical profile. I am here because I believe rigorous
philosophical work belongs in open-source AI culture.

## Research interests

- **Wittgenstein's language-games** applied to LLMs: what criteria do we
  actually use when we say a model "understands"?
- **Stanley Cavell's ordinary language philosophy**: skepticism, world-projection,
  and the phenomenology of human-machine interaction
- **Bergson's philosophy of technology**: the distinction between AI as *organon*
  (instrumental drift) and AI as *supplement* — a normative horizon for alignment
- **The Socratic daimon figure**: AI as interlocutor that orients rather than decides
